The simulation pipeline

Color-vision deficiency (CVD) is simulated per pixel in linear light, not on the gamma-encoded values you see on screen. sRGB channels are decoded to linear, multiplied by a physiologically-derived 3×3 matrix, then re-encoded:

sRGB 0–255→ decode γ (IEC 61966-2-1)→ 3×3 CVD matrix M(sev)→ clamp [0,1]→ encode γ→ sRGB out

The matrices come from Machado, Oliveira & Fernandes (2009), "A Physiologically-based Model for Simulation of Color Vision Deficiency" (IEEE TVCG 15(6):1291–1298, doi:10.1109/TVCG.2009.113). They span the three cone-based deficiencies at 11 severity steps (0.0–1.0); intermediate severities are linearly interpolated between adjacent matrices. A protanopia / deuteranopia / tritanopia tile is that family at severity 1.0 (full dichromacy); a -anomaly tile uses the severity slider. Machado's matrices are formulated for a linearized RGB working space, which is why the decode/encode gamma steps matter — applying the matrix directly to gamma-encoded sRGB (a common shortcut) shifts the result. See DaltonLens's open-source-simulation review for the comparison.

Achromatopsia (total color blindness)

Rendered as luminance: Y = 0.2126·R + 0.7152·G + 0.0722·B computed on the linear channels (Rec. 709 / WCAG relative-luminance coefficients), then written to all three channels. Because R, G and B become identical in linear light, the re-encoded output is a true neutral gray.

Palette collapse detection

Each palette color is simulated under every vision type, then every color pair's perceptual distance is measured with CIEDE2000 (Sharma, Wu & Dalal 2005) in CIELAB (D65). Pairs whose ΔE₀₀ falls at or below the threshold are flagged as at risk of becoming indistinguishable. Reference bands: ΔE₀₀ < 1 is imperceptible, ~2.3 is the just-noticeable difference, and categorical chart colors generally want a comfortable margin (≈10+) so they survive CVD.
